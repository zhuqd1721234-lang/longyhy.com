Frequently Asked Questions

The things you ask most, answered before you have to ask.

// Selection
How should I choose between TGIC and HAA curing agent?▾
For EU exports, only HAA can be used, since TGIC is now listed as a Substance of Very High Concern (SVHC) and cannot be exported to the EU. For non-EU markets like India, Turkey, the Middle East, and Southeast Asia, both systems work: TGIC has slightly better outdoor weatherability at a higher price (approx. $4,500–7,000/ton); HAA is fully compliant at a slightly lower cost (approx. $3,500–5,500/ton). If unsure, we recommend requesting samples of both and deciding based on actual curing test results — see the curing agent details page.
How do I choose between low-temperature curing and standard resin?▾
If your baking oven has high energy costs, or your substrate is heat-sensitive, choose low-temperature curing (150–160℃); for typical outdoor aluminum profile or furniture hardware applications, standard (180–200℃) is fine. Send us your curing oven process parameters for free selection advice.

Samples typically ship within 5 business days; actual arrival time depends on customs clearance efficiency at the destination. For a full bundle inquiry, all five components ship together in one batch, so your testing timeline stays synchronized.
What's the difference between a full bundle inquiry and a single-item inquiry?▾
A full bundle inquiry suits customers who need all five components sourced at once — we quote based on your formula ratio, ship samples together, and issue a bundle-wide formula compatibility verification report. A single-item inquiry suits customers who already have some suppliers and just need one or two components — no requirement to buy the full bundle. Both paths are available on the home page inquiry form.

// Quality & Risk
What happens if the goods fail testing after arrival at port?▾
Our formal contracts include a quality guarantee clause: if a third-party testing agency confirms non-conformance with contracted specs within 30 days of port arrival, we provide a full refund or replacement. See Quality Guarantee for details.
What share of total powder coating cost do raw materials represent?▾
Industry-wide, raw materials typically make up 80–90% of total powder coating production cost, which is why raw material selection and price stability have such a large impact on overall margins — this is also why we offer formula risk verification services and a price monitoring subscription.
