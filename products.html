TGIC · HAA
Powder Coating Curing Agent System

// Component 02/05 · 4–6% of formula · Cross-links with polyester resin
Curing agent is used in a small quantity but is the key reactant determining whether the formula sets properly. TGIC performs better but is EU-restricted; HAA is fully compliant but needs a slightly adjusted formula ratio. We stock both systems and recommend directly based on your target market.
TGIC vs HAA: How to Choose
Exporting to the EU: only HAA is usable, since TGIC is now listed as an SVHC substance of high concern. Exporting to India/Turkey/Middle East: both systems work — TGIC performs slightly better, HAA is slightly cheaper. We recommend stocking samples of both and switching dynamically based on your customer's end market.

Leveling + Degassing Agents
Smallest Quantity, Highest Technical Content

// Component 03/05 · 1–2% of formula · Determines surface finish
Leveling and degassing agents make up just 1–2% of the formula, but directly determine whether the film surface is smooth and free of pinholes. This is the most technically demanding part of the bundle — customers are most sensitive to this component's quality, and most willing to pay for good results.
Acrylic leveling agent, improves melt flow, eliminates orange peel and craters
Benzoin degassing agent, releases trapped gas during thick-film curing, preventing pinholes
Solid powder form, pre-mixed in the same process as resin and curing agent, non-hazardous
Free sample testing, matched directly to your existing film issues (orange peel/craters/pinholes)

Common Film Issues Reference
The surface problem you're seeing maps to a specific additive adjustment.
🟠
Orange Peel (Rough Surface)
Insufficient melt flow, leaving an uneven, orange-peel-like texture on the film surface.
Recommended: acrylic leveling agent · increase dosage by 0.2–0.3%
🕳️
Pinholes / Craters
Gas not fully released during curing, leaving tiny holes that affect appearance and corrosion resistance.
Recommended: benzoin degassing agent · essential for thick-film applications
✨
Uneven Gloss
Noticeable gloss variation within the same batch, affecting appearance consistency.
Recommended: combined leveling + degassing formula

Titanium Dioxide
Core Pigment for White & Light Formulas

// Component 04/05 · 2–5% of formula · Rutile grade · Powder coating specific
Powder coatings have different titanium dioxide requirements than liquid coatings — more focus on heat resistance (no color shift at 160–200℃ curing temps) and dispersion (uniform distribution in molten resin). We supply rutile titanium dioxide specifically formulated for powder coating processes.
Rutile chloride process, heat-resistant, non-yellowing, suited to powder coating baking
High-dispersion surface treatment, disperses uniformly during resin melt extrusion, no clumping
Same-batch SGS testing, whiteness, hiding power and heat-color-shift specs shipped with report
Blended with barium sulfate, ratio adjusted to your cost budget
